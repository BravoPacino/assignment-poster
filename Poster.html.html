ACM Principle 1.3: Be Honest and Trustworthy

üìú Full Principle Statement:

"Honesty is an essential component of trustworthiness. A computing professional should be honest about their qualifications, and about any limitations in their competence to complete a task."

What This Means for AI Use:

When students submit AI-generated text without acknowledgment, they are claiming work that is not theirs. This violates the fundamental principle of honesty.

üéØ Application to Our Scenario:

  • The Problem: Using ChatGPT to write assignments without disclosure = presenting AI capabilities as your own
  • Why It Matters: You're telling your lecturer "I wrote this" when you didn't
  • The Violation: This is dishonest representation of your work and competence

‚úÖ Ethical Alternative:

Be transparent about AI use: "This draft was developed with assistance from ChatGPT for brainstorming and structure. Final analysis and synthesis are my own work."

Remember: Professional integrity starts with honesty about what you can and cannot do independently.

ACM Principle 1.5: Respect the Work Required to Produce New Ideas

üìú Full Principle Statement:

"Developing new ideas, inventions, creative works, and computing artifacts creates value for society, and those who expend this effort should expect to gain value from their work. Computing professionals should therefore credit the creators of ideas, inventions, work, and artifacts, and respect copyrights, patents, trade secrets, license agreements, and other methods of protecting authors' works."

What This Means for AI Use:

When you use AI shortcuts without disclosure, you disrespect both the learning process and peers who did the work themselves.

üéØ Application to Our Scenario:

  • Respect for Learning: Education requires effort to develop skills and understanding
  • Respect for Peers: Your classmates who work independently deserve fair evaluation
  • Respect for Process: Critical thinking and analysis skills require practice and struggle
  • Respect for Attribution: AI-generated content should be acknowledged, just like any other source

‚ö†Ô∏è What Gets Disrespected:

  • The intellectual effort required to master concepts
  • The time and work your classmates invested
  • The educational process designed to develop your capabilities
  • Your own future professional competence
Key Insight: Using AI without acknowledgment doesn't just disrespect the tool‚Äîit disrespects the entire learning process and everyone engaged in it honestly.

  • Engineers must maintain professional standards in all work
  • Integrity is non-negotiable in technical professions

7. To seek, accept, and offer honest criticism of technical work, to acknowledge and correct errors
